Sensible DateTime

Sensible DateTime is a jQuery plugin that makes it easy to format ISO datetime into something sensible as you see fit.

Intro

There are a handful of Javascript libraries that convert date on your page to relative time or format them. However, they are usually set in their predefined way.

Sensible DateTime provides more flexibility that helps format them according to your specific needs and context.

Example

Original markups

<p>You opened this page <abbr class="datetime opened">opened time</abbr>.<!-- dynamically generated --></p>
<p>Someone posted something <time class="datetime" datetime="2011-09-27T15:40:00">2011, Sep 27th</time></p>
<p>John shared his photo <time class="datetime" datetime="2011-09-26T11:42:11">9-26-2011</time></p>
<p>Jane closed issue 12 <time class="datetime" datetime="2011-09-21T02:27:42">2011, September 21</time></p>
<p>Harry started using jQuery <abbr class="datetime" title="2011-08-12T14:51:03">Aug 12</abbr></p>
<p>Sent an email <time class="datetime" datetime="2001-12-25T11:21:53">2011 12/25</time></p>
<p>My birthday is <abbr class="datetime" title="1984-05-15T00:00:00">May 15th, 1984</abbr></p>

How to use

Download latest version here (last updated 2011, Sep 27th)

If you use tags other than time to markup the datetime. The tag should have ISO8601 datetime string as the value of title attribute.

HTML5 introduced a new tag called time that has datetime attribute. You can use the new time tag with ISO8601 datetime string as the value of datetime attribute.

Optionally, you can pass an object to override the default settings.

  • refreshRate: how often do auto-update in milliseconds
  • pastMask: used for any datetime that is more past than a year (by default) or what you specified
  • futureMask: used for any datetime that is in future
  • masks: array of objects that specify the distance of seconds from the current time and how to display datetime within that distance

You can use any combination of flags from the list below to specify how you want to display datetime in your mask string. Or none, if you don't want to :(

  • %d - the day of the month (1-31)
  • %dd - the day of the month (00-31)
  • %ddd - the short name of the day (Mon - Sun)
  • %dddd - the long name of the day (Monday - Sunday)
  • %m - the month (1 - 12)
  • %mm - the month (01 - 12)
  • %mmm - the short name of the month (Jan - Dec)
  • %mmmm - the long name of the month (January - December)
  • %yy - the year (00 - 99)
  • %yyyy - the year (0000 - 9999)
  • %h - the hour (0 - 12)
  • %hh - the hour (00 - 12)
  • %H - the hour (0 - 24)
  • %HH - the hour (00 - 12)
  • %M - the minutes (0 - 59)
  • %MM - the minutes (00 - 59)
  • %s - the seconds (0 - 59)
  • %ss - the seconds (00 - 59)
  • %t - a or p
  • %tt - am or pm
  • %T - A or P
  • %TT - AM or PM
  • %S - Ordinal of the day (st, nd, rd, th)
  • %xs - distance from now in seconds
  • %xm - distance from now in minutes
  • %xh - distance from now in hours
  • %xd - distance from now in days
  • %xy - distance from now in years
